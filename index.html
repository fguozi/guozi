Much to my house-proud mother's chagrin. I spent my childhood hoarding strange objects. These ranged from a handcrafted plate-lifter inherited from relatives, to a honey dipper I hid beneath my pillow. Years later, I was privileged enough to attend SOTA and graduated with a diploma in Visual Arts. There, I had then discovered, amongst other things, an interest in anthropology— watching people interacting with things. It was only in Industrial Design where my collection of strange objects grew alongside my semantic understanding and appreciation of their presence in the everyday condition. I revered local designers' Atelier HOKO's Science of The Secondary book series and binge- watched SPACE10's webinars, finding their artistic flair in curbing the mundanity of everyday life inspirational. I long to create the 'Super Normal'-esque objects that ensnared me as a child, but yet I could not aptly describe my dream job title. So I’m still figuring that out. Amidst attending university at NUS DID and watching people doing everyday things like a stalker.
